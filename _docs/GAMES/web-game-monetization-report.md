# Web Game Monetization Report

**Date:** 2026-06-22
**Context:** Side income strategy analysis for web-based games vs niche SaaS

## Executive Summary

Web-based games can generate meaningful side income, but the economics are highly skewed. Top performers on platforms like Poki can earn up to €1 million annually, but this represents the top 1% of games. Most web games earn minimal revenue unless they achieve viral success.

For comparison, niche SaaS applications (like BellRack for kettlebell athletes) offer a more predictable path to sustainable income with lower risk and effort.

## Platform Analysis

### Poki

**Model:** Revenue share (typically 50/50 split of ad revenue)

**Scale:**
- 100 million monthly players
- 1 billion plays per month
- 600+ independent studios
- 1,000+ curated games

**Earnings:**
- **Top performers:** Up to €1 million (~$1.1M USD) annually
- **Historical growth:** Top studio revenues increased tenfold over 5 years (from $50k to $1M)
- **Mid-tier:** Likely $50k-$200k/year for successful but not top-tier games
- **Majority:** Most games earn far less

**Success Case Study - Emolingo Games:**
- Turkish indie studio: 2 → 5 full-time employees on Poki revenue alone
- "Rainbow Obby": 100+ million plays
- 800,000 daily plays across 8-game catalog
- 3 months development time per game
- 2-3 game releases per year

**Requirements:**
- Quality threshold - games are curated, not auto-accepted
- Consistent updates and new releases
- Web exclusivity offers better rates but limits distribution

### itch.io

**Model:** Optional payments, tips, revenue share on sales

**Earnings:**
- Typically <$100/month unless you have a hit
- Better for visibility and community building than monetization
- Low barrier to entry, but low ceiling for most developers

### Own Website

**Model:** Full control of ads/monetization (AdSense, direct ads, in-game purchases)

**Earnings:**
- Full revenue (no platform cut)
- But requires marketing and traffic acquisition
- Most earn <$50/month initially without established audience
- Ad rates: $0.50-$5 per 1,000 views (CPM)

## Key Challenges

### Web Games

1. **Market Saturation**
   - Krunker, Narrow.one, and many others dominate
   - High quality bar due to established competitors
   - Players have endless free options

2. **User Acquisition**
   - Expensive to drive traffic to own site
   - Platform curation is competitive
   - Viral success is unpredictable

3. **Technical Complexity**
   - Multiplayer networking
   - Anti-cheat systems
   - Server hosting costs
   - Cross-browser compatibility

4. **Monetization Limitations**
   - Ad revenue requires massive volume
   - In-game purchases need active user base
   - Free competitors limit pricing power

### Niche SaaS (BellRack Comparison)

**Advantages:**
- Targeted audience with specific pain points
- Higher willingness to pay ($5-$20/month)
- Lower technical complexity (no multiplayer/anti-cheat)
- Predictable customer acquisition through niche channels
- Subscription model = recurring revenue

**Example Calculation:**
- 500 users × $10/month = $60,000/year
- Achievable with focused marketing in kettlebell community
- No viral success required

## Strategic Recommendations

### For Web Game Development

**Only pursue if:**
- You have a unique, innovative game concept
- You enjoy game development as a passion project
- You're willing to treat it as a lottery ticket (high risk, high reward)
- You have marketing skills or budget for user acquisition

**Best approach:**
- Start with simple, polished games (3-month dev cycle like Emolingo)
- Focus on one platform initially (Poki for revenue, itch.io for feedback)
- Plan for multiple game releases (portfolio approach)
- Reinvest earnings into marketing and better games

### For Niche SaaS (BellRack Path)

**Advantages:**
- More predictable income trajectory
- Lower technical risk
- Targeted marketing is cheaper and more effective
- Subscription model builds recurring revenue
- Can bootstrap without external funding

**Strategy:**
- Focus on niche audience (kettlebell athletes)
- Solve specific pain points (workout tracking, PR tracking)
- Price for value ($10-$20/month)
- Use content marketing and community building
- Iterate based on user feedback

## Conclusion

Web games can be profitable, but the economics are extremely skewed. The top 1% earn substantial income, but the majority earn minimal returns. Success requires either exceptional game quality, viral marketing, or consistent portfolio building.

Niche SaaS offers a more reliable path to side income with lower risk and effort. For someone with domain expertise (like kettlebell training), building a targeted solution for that community is likely to yield better returns with less uncertainty than competing in the saturated web game market.

**Recommendation:** Focus on BellRack and niche SaaS opportunities. Treat web game development as a passion project or secondary pursuit, not as a primary income strategy.
