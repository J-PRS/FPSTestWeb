Based on the search results, there's no official documented origin, but the community explanation is:

**"Krunk"** is slang for "very drunk" (like "crunk"). So **"Krunker"** would mean "drunker" - implying the players are drunk, which kind of makes sense given the chaotic, fast-paced gameplay.

The name follows the popular **.io** naming convention for browser games (like Agar.io, Slither.io, MooMoo.io) that was trending when Krunker launched in May 2018.

**Developer:** Sidney de Vries (Yendis Entertainment)
**Released:** May 20, 2018
**Other games:** vertix.io, driftin.io, bloble.io, karnage.io, foes.io, moomoo.io

So it's essentially a made-up word combining "krunk" (drunk) + ".io" (browser game suffix) = chaotic drunk shooter. Fits the vibe.
